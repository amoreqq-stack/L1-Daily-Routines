GSimple Present Tense - Rules & Structure
*We use Simple Present for habits, routines, and facts. All the phrases in this lesson use Simple Present - so learning the rule helps you use them correctly.
Positive
I / You / We / They + verb
I wake up at 7.
They finish work at 5.
We take a break at noon.
Negative
I / You + don't + verb
I don't wake up early.
She doesn't check emails.
He doesn't work from home.
Question
Do + I/you/we + verb?  |  Does + he/she/it + verb?
Do you take a break at noon?   Yes, I do. / No, I don't.
Does she work from home?   Yes, she does. / No, she doesn't.
He / She / It - add S
He / She / It + verb + S
He wakes up early.   She checks emails.   My boss finishes work late.

Signal words - these tell you to use Simple Present:

TipIn this lesson we focus on I - the most natural form for talking about your own routine. He/She forms will come up in the main task when describing someone else's day.
